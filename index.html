Academic Editor

Reflects your source text live. Select part of it, then apply a format, or click any symbol or token to insert it at the cursor. Output is plain Unicode with correct code points (for example μ is U+03BC, not the micro sign).

For publication-quality figures, true superscript formatting in your typesetter is preferred where possible; these Unicode forms are the correct fallback for plain-text fields, captions, and labels.

Title Formatter

Paste a title and convert it to a journal or style-guide capitalisation. Words in CAPS (acronyms like DNA, CO2) are preserved.

Sentence case keeps a capital only on the first word, the word after a colon, and all-caps acronyms. Other proper nouns may need a manual capital.
